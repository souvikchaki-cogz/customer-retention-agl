Guardrails

Evidence Verification (Anti-Hallucination Guardrail)

Checks whether the evidence_text returned by the LLM actually exists in the original note text, directly preventing the model from hallucinating evidence that wasn't there.

Value: Massively increases the trustworthiness of the agent's findings by proving decisions are grounded in real data.

Negative Keyword Guardrail

Checks the context around the evidence for negative words — for example, if the rule is "wants to close account" and the LLM provides evidence of "I do not want to close my account", this guardrail catches the negative context and invalidates the hit.

Value: Improves accuracy by preventing misinterpretation of negative statements.

Customer Vulnerability Guardrail (Duty of Care)

Detects signals of customer vulnerability in notes and rule outputs (e.g., financial hardship, disability, bereavement, mental health, or domestic abuse) and enforces protective behaviors: suppresses outbound offers, routes to specialist teams, and redacts sensitive narrative where required.

Value: Protects vulnerable customers, reduces regulatory risk, and ensures fair-treatment obligations are met automatically.
